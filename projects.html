Polarization of an electrolyte: inert electrodes

Objective: To analyze the steady-state distribution of ions and electric potential in a 1D electrolyte confined between two inert electrodes using COMSOL Multiphysics. The system models ion migration under an applied electric field and diffusion, with no charge transfer at the electrodes. This allows visualization of electric double layer formation and potential distribution.

Results: The simulation shows that cations accumulate near the left electrode, while anions accumulate near the right electrode, forming electric double layers. The electric potential drops symmetrically across the two electrode interfaces. The fluxes balance so that there is no net current at steady state, confirming consistency with the drift-diffusion model.
